# 🤝 Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## 🌟 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the **Resume Analyzer AI** community pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone. We embrace diversity and are committed to maintaining a welcoming, inclusive, and professional environment, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community, driving the future of ethical and transparent AI recruitment tools.

---

## 🎯 Our Standards

### ✅ Positive Behaviors
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive, collaborative environment for our community include:

*   **💖 Demonstrating Empathy:** Showing kindness and understanding toward other people, recognizing that contributors come from all over the world with different technical backgrounds.
*   **🤝 Being Respectful:** Respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences during code reviews and architectural discussions.
*   **🛠️ Graceful Feedback:** Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback. Code reviews are about the code, not the person.
*   **🌱 Accountability:** Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience to improve.
*   **🌍 Community First:** Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall health and longevity of the open-source community.

### 🚫 Unacceptable Behaviors
Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:

*   **❌ Inappropriate Language:** The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
*   **🛑 Harassment:** Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks against any member.
*   **👁️ Privacy Violations:** Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission (Doxing).
*   **⚠️ Unprofessional Conduct:** Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional open-source software engineering setting.

---

## ⚖️ Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and core maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and absolute responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, GitHub Issues, and Pull Requests that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct. They will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## 🌐 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (GitHub, Discord, Slack, etc.), and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline tech event.

---

## 🚨 Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement by sending a direct email to the repository maintainers. 

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident. Retaliation against any individual who brings forward a complaint will result in immediate banning.

---

## 📋 Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction 📝
*   **Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.
*   **Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested depending on the severity.

### 2. Warning ⚠️
*   **Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or a series of minor actions.
*   **Consequence**: A strict warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time.

### 3. Temporary Ban 🛑
*   **Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior or aggressive code-review conduct.
*   **Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community repository for a specified period of time (e.g., 30 days).

### 4. Permanent Ban 🚫
*   **Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward classes of individuals.
*   **Consequence**: A permanent, irreversible ban from any sort of public interaction within the community and immediate closure of any open Pull Requests authored by the individual.
